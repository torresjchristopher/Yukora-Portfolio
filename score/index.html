The T.C.A.P. Engine

Intelligence is a cost. The algorithm discerns precisely where artificial reasoning creates leverage, and where deterministic computation already wins.

01_IDENTIFY

Enumerate every process that consumes time, compute, memory, tokens, or coordination overhead.

02_CLASSIFY

Determine predictability and reasoning requirement. Withhold intelligence from tasks that don't need it.

03_SCORE

Establish a measurable baseline for runtime behavior and the computational cost of the required result.

04_DRIVE_DOWN

Feed the result of each cycle into the next until the process reaches a lower stable operating floor.

Dynamic Aggregation

The score changes when the business changes. It is a compositional metric; its structure follows the actual scope of the operating system it measures.

+
Addition
A newly discovered workflow creates a new angle and enters the aggregate with its own baseline.
÷
Fractionalization
One broad workflow separates into independently measurable subprocesses for precise optimization.
↔
Consolidation
Redundant operations merge into one lower-cost process and one accountable score.
−
Elimination
When a process produces no necessary value, its optimal score is zero. It ceases to run.
